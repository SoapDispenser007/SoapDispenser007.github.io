Getting Started

Here you can get information to start your very own 'Treasure Chest.' You can learn how to set up your very own tank and care for your new fish, shrimp, snails, and plants!

Fish

Fish are the core of every tank, if you're just starting out guppies are your best friends. They're inexpensive, easy to care for and are very forgiving when it comes to mistakes.

Guppies come in all different colors and shapes allowing you to add a splash of color and personalization to your tank. Be careful though, store bought guppies are often inbred and come with diseases. Buy from local fish keepers or hobbyists when you can.

Shrimp

Shrimp are the colorful little bottom feeders that keep your tank clean. While not essential to your tank they certainly add a new level of livelyness to your tank. It is best to start with Neocardinia shrimp, or more commonly called cherry shrimp, they come in a varitey of bright colors like red, green, blue, and more!

Shrimp need lots of cover and hiding places to feel safe so moss and caves are a great addition to make your shrimp feel safe.

Snails

Snails are the little janitors of your tank who scale walls and eat stuff you can't see. They often hitchhike onto live plants and commonly pop up even when you weren't expecting them. Don't worry, they wont do any harm to your tank.

Snails will reproduce with an abundance of food so be careful to not overfeed your fish becasue they get to snack on the leftovers and suddenly you have 40+ snails in your tank.

Extra information

Tank sizes

A common rule for fish tanks are you need a minimum of 5 gallons and you can keep up to 1 fish per gallon of water. This basically means if I have a 10 gallon tank I could essentially fit 10, 1 inch guppies. However it is not advised to stock your tank fully especially if you have other creatures like shrimp and snails. I currently have a 10 gallon tank with natural decorations, 4 guppies, shrimp, and snails. These parameters work perfectly for a well balanced ecosystem

Plants and their benefits

As explained, live plants not only look pretty but also generate oxygen, consume carbon dioxide, ammonia, nitrates, and nitrites, that are produced by fish waste. They also are able to consume nutrients that are essential for algae, which can quickly overtake your tank, preventing and overgrowth of algae. These simple looking plants keep your tank water clean and safe for your fish.
